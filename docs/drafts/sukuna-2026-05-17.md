This is not a one-off — it's the recurring failure mode: the Service Rule enforces HTTP-side contract truth, but nothing enforces that human-facing instructions stay aligned with the contract. Candidate for a Layer 1 doc-currency check: a Service Rule for memory/.
- **Decision 022 doesn't follow the canon decision format, and it was produced by the loop that's supposed to keep canon coherent.** No frontmatter, missing `Status`/`Date`/`Context`/`Consequences`, divergent title style. The decision approval ritual approved the content, not the structure. Doctrine-service hasn't shipped, so there's no automated check; the human-judgment boundary at `prismo review` didn't catch it either. Synthesis-service's prompt template is silently teaching the model "Prismo decisions look like this" using whichever shape the LLM defaulted to.

## 3. Thinking

- **Decision 022 is already the first dogfood failure of Decision 022.** It names "adapter ontology drift" as the smell where adapter assumptions calcify into ontology — and the synthesis-service prompt template silently encoded the LLM's preferred markdown shape (no frontmatter, casual title) as the canon-decision contract. That's adapter ontology drift, fourth instance, captured live in the decision the loop just emitted. Fix is small (update the synthesis prompt to include the canon frontmatter schema, or make doctrine-service's metadata-reconciliation a synthesis precondition). The interesting question is whether 022 should be re-emitted in the corrected format or annotated — annotating is honest, re-emitting is cleaner. Lean: annotate, because the malformed first emission is itself the evidence the smell is real.

- **The Sukuna script (this script) is the loudest live violation of the filesystem-for-durable-truth principle.** Decision 021: "the filesystem does NOT contain operational limbo states / pre-approval drafts / half-authored workflow intermediates." This document is exactly that. Sukuna's next iteration should `POST /synthesis/run` (or directly `POST /review/queue` one ReviewItem per finding) and emit nothing to disk. The work to extract is non-trivial because Sukuna's value has been the narrative shape — but Section 1's diffs are ReviewItem-shaped already (one item per fix), Section 2's observations are ReviewItem-shaped (one item per observation, artifact_type=annotation), and Section 3 maps to artifact_type=proposed-idea or annotation-to-decision. The Sukuna 2026-05-17 report should be the last one written to `drafts/`.

- **The 6 promoted-but-not-authored captures should re-enter the loop as captures, not stay as drafts.** Decision 021's prescription ("leave them; author manually if desired; or treat as synthesis fuel") is three mutually-exclusive options labeled as one. The cleanest move: re-POST their content to `/workflow/capture` as fresh signals, mark the draft files as historical, and let synthesis-service eat them in the observation week. That tests the loop on real material and resolves the orphan-in-place state. Cost: ~6 curl calls. Benefit: removes the only "see, the new model works for new captures but old ones rot" exception. The alternative is letting six structurally-valuable signals — including capture `1801ff71` (V2-pure reframe, foundational) — sit in legacy storage forever.

- **`mcp.md` not listing the new tools is the architecture quietly excluding Claude from the contract Decision 021 says is universal.** "Every Layer 4 surface is a thin ReviewItem renderer + action handler against the same contract." MCP is a Layer 4 surface. If MCP doesn't wrap `/review/queue/*`, then Claude-in-session cannot triage ReviewItems via MCP — only via shell-out to the CLI. That's a coupling break: the assistant most likely to *cause* ReviewItem emission (via captures) cannot see what was emitted without leaving its native surface. Either ship `list_review_queue` + the four review-action MCP tools immediately, or explicitly state in Decision 021 that MCP-as-Layer-4 is non-goal (which contradicts the "every surface" framing). Probably the former.

- **Confidence ≥ 0.7 is theatre and the first run proved it.** Two ReviewItems emitted at 0.82 and 0.75, both passed. Both became canon (one decision, one annotation). The "confidence" the synthesis-service reports is the LLM's self-reported certainty about its own proposal — not a measure of whether the proposal conforms to canon shape, doesn't duplicate an existing decision, fits the right artifact type, or has the right level of evidence. Decision 022's structural defects are evidence: the LLM was confident, the proposal was structurally non-conformant, the gate let it through. Replace the confidence threshold with (a) structural pre-validation (frontmatter present + valid; canon path naming; supersession integrity), and (b) keep confidence as a logged-but-not-gating signal until observation tells us what confidence actually correlates with. Confidence-as-gate is the same shape as the two-week-soak gate: a substitute for not having a real test.

- **`rejection_reason` enumerated from day one is a soak in different clothing.** Decision 021 makes this explicit ("without categorized rejections, synthesis-service tuning is guesswork") — the categories are: `redundant | too-weak | already-represented | incorrect-interpretation | wrong-artifact-type | too-speculative | not-durable | other`. Seven categories pre-observation. Decision 019's lesson ("production-state-dependent gates are a code smell — behavior belongs in tests, not soaks") applies symmetrically to ontology guessed before observation. The right move: ship with `rejection_reason` as a free-text field for the observation week, cluster the actual reasons given, then enumerate from the clusters. Otherwise you'll get `other` for everything that doesn't fit the seven, and you'll never know which categories are doing work.

- **The build accidentally encoded adapter assumptions as ontology in two places.** (a) `ReviewItem.proposal.frontmatter` is a dict-shaped first-class field — that locks the data model to YAML-frontmatter-shaped artifacts. A future artifact type that doesn't have frontmatter (a runbook patch, a recent-changes-entry, an annotation diff) either bleeds adapter assumption into ontology or gets a `frontmatter: null` carve-out (which is the smell). (b) `ReviewItem.proposal.suggested_destination` is a filesystem path — but ReviewItems are supposed to be surface-agnostic. A WhatsApp surface has no filesystem destination; a web UI doesn't render `decisions/022-...md` to a user. Both are workstation adapter assumptions wearing contract clothing. Worth surfacing while the schema is still soft.

- **Decision-write cadence (4 in 48 hours) exceeded any plausible reflection cadence, and the supersession graph is now load-bearing.** Decision 021 supersedes 012, deprecates the promote step, freezes drafts, resequences 020, narrows Sukuna scope, and adds 5 endpoints — six load-bearing claims bundled as "the consolidation decision." That label discourages re-examination of each piece. Then Decision 022 was emitted by the loop Decision 021 defined, *during* the observation week Decision 021 also defined. The decisions are referencing each other across hours. Proposed countermeasure: declare a one-week decision freeze. No new decisions, only ReviewItems and rejections. Use the rejection data to write Decision 023 (synthesis tuning) empirically rather than in another 48-hour burst.

- **The "premature lock-in" risk is highest in the ReviewItem schema.** Decision 021 calls the ReviewItem "the universal Layer 4 integration primitive" — meaning every future surface depends on its shape. The schema has shipped after one (1) live synthesis run with two emitted items. Fields like `artifact_type` (5 enumerated values), `rejection_reason` (7 enumerated values), `status` (5 enumerated values) are all pre-observation guesses. Decision 020's "Module-first, service-later" reasoning applies: keep the schema in flux until the observation week is real (≥ 50 ReviewItems across ≥ 3 artifact types). The minimum-viable countermeasure is a `schema_version` field on every ReviewItem so migrations are explicit when the inevitable schema bumps happen.

- **One under-examined commitment from the rush: doctrine-service moved to post-approval guardrail.** Decision 020 had doctrine-service shipping first as the structural-coherence engine; Decision 021 deferred it on the rationale that "until synthesis-service exists, doctrine-service has no upstream loop that benefits from it." That reasoning is actually backwards for Decision 022's case: if doctrine-service had shipped first (frontmatter validation, supersession integrity, metadata reconciliation), Decision 022's structural defects would have been caught before approval, not after. The current order makes synthesis-service the loudest writer to canon with no structural validator running. Consider: ship a 50-line doctrine-service pre-flight check (frontmatter present + Status/Date/Context/Decision/Rationale/Consequences sections present + title format matches) as a synthesis-service post-condition. Five-percent of the Decision 020 scope, prevents 90% of the structural defects the observation week will otherwise catch by hand.
